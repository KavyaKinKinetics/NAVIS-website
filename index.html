The Challenge

Orbital congestion is now a governance problem

Operators have conjunction alerts and tracking systems. What they lack is a structured, quantitative framework for determining when a satellite should exit orbit.

End-of-life decisions are made today across disconnected silos — with no unified model to arbitrate the trade-offs between engineering, compliance, and economic risk.

I
Engineering
Monitors fuel and subsystem health — but ignores economic value and regulatory timing constraints entirely.
II
Compliance
Tracks static rules (the 25-year rule, now 5-year) without dynamic stochastic risk assessment.
III
Finance
Models asset depreciation but lacks orbital context for risk-adjusted exposure quantification.
Result: disconnected silos. No unified model. Suboptimal disposal timing and compounding liability risk.
The Framework

Six layers.
One auditable output.

A deterministic decision architecture. Each layer contributes a quantified signal to the final governance recommendation.

Layer 01
Satellite State Data
Telemetry, fuel reserves, battery health, mission age, CAM count, and orbital parameters from the satellite bus. The raw signal layer.
Layer 02
Regulatory Compliance Gates
IADC thresholds and evolving mandates (5-year rule, FCC standards) encoded as live decision logic — not static policy documents.
Layer 03
Disposal Success Probability
Reliability modelling of post-mission disposal maneuvers. Forecasts degradation using fuel levels, battery state, and orbital decay trends.
Layer 04
Collision Risk Quantification
Estimates the probability of a debris collision event based on orbital altitude, object density in the surrounding shell, exposure duration, and secondary fragmentation risk.
Layer 05
Decision & Economic Exposure
All signals synthesised into a risk score. Quantifies expected collision loss, disposal failure cost, and asset exposure to produce a scored, explainable recommendation.
Layer 06
Governance & Audit Trail
Every decision is logged, timestamped, and structured for three audiences: regulators (compliance evidence), insurers (premium classification), and operators (action plan).
