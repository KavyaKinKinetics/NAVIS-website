The Challenge

Orbital Congestion Is No Longer a Tracking Problem. It Is a Governance Problem.

Low Earth Orbit is scaling rapidly. Constellations are increasing orbital density, regulatory scrutiny is tightening, and insurers are reducing exposure to space risk.

Operators have conjunction alerts and tracking systems — but no structured framework that determines the optimal disposal timing of a satellite.

Today, end-of-life decisions are fragmented across engineering, compliance, and finance — without a unified quantitative model.

01

Engineering

Focuses on fuel levels and technical health, often ignoring economic value and orbital context.

02

Compliance

Tracks static rules (e.g., 25-year rule) without dynamic risk assessment or stochastic modeling.

03

Finance

Models depreciation but lacks orbital context for risk-adjusted valuation and collision probability.

Critical Gap: Disconnected silos leading to fragmented, suboptimal disposal decisions

The Solution

The NAVIS Framework

NAVIS operates as a deterministic five-layer decision architecture integrating telemetry, regulatory encoding, probabilistic disposal modeling, stochastic collision intensity estimation, and economic exposure optimization into a single auditable output.

05

Decision & Economic Exposure

Quantifies the financial impact of disposal timing, balancing residual value against liability risk in a unified optimization model.

04

Stochastic Congestion & Event Modeling

Estimates time-dependent collision event intensity using stochastic modeling, incorporating orbital density, exposure duration, and encounter rates to quantify systemic cascade risk.

03

Disposal Success Probability

Models reliability of post-mission disposal maneuvers based on historical data, satellite health, and propulsion system status.

02

Regulatory Compliance Gates

Explicitly encodes evolving rules (e.g., 5-year vs 25-year) into the decision logic with automated compliance verification.

01

Satellite State Data

Ingests telemetry, fuel reserves, and operational status from the satellite bus in real-time.
